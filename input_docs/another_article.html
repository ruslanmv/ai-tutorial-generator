Learning activities for university students

Students, skill up to be competitive and prepare for the future of work with free, industry-relevant courses in Gen AI.

  • Introduction to Large Language Models
    Course ~ 1 hour and 30 minutes
    Explore the capabilities of large language models and their business applications. You'll learn about the different types of IBM Granite models and the unique features that make them ideal for enterprise use. You'll also discover how to craft effective prompts to guide these models and overcome common challenges in their use.
  • IBM Granite Models for Software Development
    Course ~ 1 hour and 30 minutes
    Discover how generative artificial intelligence is rewriting the rules of code creation. You'll learn about IBM Granite models and discover their different types and the benefits they bring to your coding journey. You'll also learn how to use IBM Granite models to troubleshoot code, allowing you to identify and fix issues faster.
  • Code Generation and Optimization Using IBM Granite
    Course ~ 3 hours and 30 minutes
    Explore how to use IBM Granite models to address a variety of programming challenges. You'll learn how to generate efficient, reliable, and adaptable code tailored to your project's needs and optimize the code to improve its performance.

Explore UN SDG 8: Decent work and economic growth

Promote productive employment, economic growth, and decent work for all
Global economic slowdowns over the past few years have made it harder for individuals to find and maintain decent work. While unemployment rates may decline, two billion workers around the world are in less stable, informal jobs without social protection.1 One in four young people are not on track to find decent work; they are not in education, employment, or training.2 This global goal is about finding ways for societies to increase and improve employment opportunities, especially for young people, reduce informal employment and labour market inequality, promote safe and secure working environments, and streamline people's access to financial services.3 Different countries and communities have their own unique issues to address, so solutions should be relevant to the specific needs of the people they are designed to help. Job creation and support for workers can have a ripple effect to help local economies, so achieving these goals for decent work can in turn ensure sustained and inclusive economic growth for the broader community.
